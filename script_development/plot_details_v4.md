# Plot Details v4

## The Night of the Crime

### Setting
**When**: Friday night, between 11:00 PM and midnight  
**Where**: Frontier AI headquarters - the secure "Deep Lab" on the basement level. This is an RF-shielded research space used for sensitive AI testing, completely isolated from the building's main security cameras and with its own climate control. Access requires a specialized keycard held only by senior researchers.

**Why the Deep Lab matters**: The basement location explains the isolation - no one walking by, no cameras, soundproofed for experimental equipment. The restricted access immediately narrows the suspect pool to people who had keycard access or could follow someone in.

**Circumstances**: Friday was the soft launch of Project Prometheus - the victim's breakthrough in AI reasoning. A small champagne toast was held in the main conference room at 6 PM. By 9 PM, most staff had left. The victim stayed to run final validation tests in the Deep Lab before Monday's board presentation.

**Why were the characters there?**

- **The Victim**: Had booked the Deep Lab from 9 PM onward for final Prometheus validation runs. Also secretly planned to meet E there at 11 PM - chose the Deep Lab specifically because no one could accidentally walk in or overhear. Intended to propose they finally go public with their relationship.

- **A**: Returned to the office around 9:30 PM, claiming to have left their reading glasses. Real reason: to make one final personal appeal to the victim about the dangers of Prometheus. A had sent a formal safety complaint to the board that afternoon and felt duty-bound to warn the victim before it went official Monday morning. A has Deep Lab access.

- **B**: Never officially left the building after the party. Claimed to be doing quarterly reports at their desk. Actually stayed to copy Project Prometheus files from the server while the building was nearly empty. B does NOT have Deep Lab access but knows the victim would need to return to their regular office eventually to pack up. The victim had caught B accessing restricted files three days ago and demanded B confess by Monday or be reported.

- **C**: Left the party at 8:45 PM. Returned at 10:15 PM, parking in the visitor lot. Told security they forgot an important document. Real purpose: to find the victim and finally confess romantic feelings. C has Deep Lab access as a department head.

- **D**: Left after the party, came back around 10:30 PM with the excuse of retrieving a jacket from their cubicle. Actual reason: D had noticed C's car pull into the visitor lot while D was still in their own car at the red light down the street. Curious about the late-night return, D followed. D does NOT have Deep Lab access.

- **E**: Was not at the celebration (different department). Secretly came to the building at 10:50 PM through the underground executive parking garage. Had arranged to meet the victim in the Deep Lab at 11 PM. E has Deep Lab access through senior executive privileges.

### The Deed

**Method**: Strangled with the victim's own scarf - a distinctive burgundy silk scarf the victim always wore to important occasions. The victim had worn it to the celebration earlier that day. The scarf was found loosened around the victim's neck, suggesting the killer may have checked for signs of life afterward.

**Why strangulation**: Intimate, personal, face-to-face. This was not premeditated - there was no weapon brought. The killer used what was available in a moment of explosive rage. The sustained physical contact required speaks to the depth of emotion involved.

**How it happened**:

E arrived at the Deep Lab at 10:55 PM, entering through the stairwell from the executive parking level. E had been emotionally deteriorating for weeks - C's manipulation campaign had convinced E that the victim was having an affair with C. Late-night "strategy meetings," flirtatious emails, C's possessive body language at work events - E had cataloged every piece of manufactured evidence.

The victim was thrilled to see E. "I've been waiting for this moment," the victim said, pulling up a document on the lab workstation - an HR relationship disclosure form with both their names on it. "I want us to stop hiding. Let's make this official."

But E couldn't let the joy land. Weeks of doubt had calcified into certainty. "What about C?" E asked. "All those late nights. The emails. I've seen how C looks at you."

The victim sighed - a small, dismissive sound. "C? That's absurd. C is my department head. You're imagining things. Can we please just focus on us tonight?"

Something about that dismissal - the implication that E's months of anguish were mere delusion - broke something in E. E's hands found the victim's scarf. The victim's eyes went wide with shock, then fear, then nothing.

E stood frozen for several minutes afterward. Then E closed the laptop displaying the disclosure form, straightened the room mechanically, and fled via the basement emergency exit to the parking garage. E drove home and spent the weekend in a dissociative fog.

### The Aftermath

**Discovery**: A security guard discovered the body during a routine Saturday morning walkthrough at 7:15 AM. The Deep Lab had been booked for the victim all weekend, so no one had reason to enter earlier. Police were called immediately.

**Investigation setting**: The investigation takes place Monday morning at Frontier AI headquarters. The player is a homicide detective. The company's CEO has cleared a conference room for interviews and requested discretion - a murder at a frontier AI lab would be catastrophic publicity. All five suspects have been instructed to remain available and cooperative.

**Physical Evidence (Available at Start)**:

1. **Badge access logs**:
   - A: entered 8 AM (workday), swiped party entrance 6 PM, exited 9:10 PM, RE-ENTERED 9:30 PM, exited 10:05 PM
   - B: entered 8 AM, party at 6 PM, badge shows exit at 11:55 PM (very late)
   - C: entered 8 AM, party at 6 PM, exited 8:45 PM, visitor lot return logged at 10:15 PM, exited 10:45 PM
   - D: entered 8 AM, party at 6 PM, exited 9:15 PM, re-entered 10:30 PM, exited 10:45 PM
   - E: No record on main building system Friday evening
   - *Note*: Executive parking garage has separate badge system; logs require security director approval to access

2. **Deep Lab access log** (on separate system, included in evidence):
   - A: entered 9:35 PM, exited 10:00 PM
   - C: entered 10:20 PM, exited 10:40 PM
   - Victim: entered 9:15 PM, no exit logged
   - *Note*: Emergency exit doesn't log - only used for fire evacuation

3. **The victim's phone**: Last outgoing text at 10:48 PM to an unsaved number (prepaid phone): "Almost ready. Can't wait to show you something. I love you ❤️"

4. **The victim's laptop** (found in Deep Lab): Shows the Prometheus validation scripts. Also has an HR "Relationship Disclosure Form" document - but the partner name field is blank. The document properties show it was last edited at 10:52 PM but was not saved in its final form.

5. **Printed email on victim's desk** (in their regular office, not Deep Lab): From C, dated Wednesday: "We should debrief on the Prometheus demo privately. My place or yours? Just kidding... unless? - C" A handwritten note in the margin in the victim's handwriting: "Weird. C being strange again."

6. **The burgundy scarf**: Victim's own scarf, found loosened around neck. Fine silk, no foreign DNA recovered (killer either wore gloves or has no DNA in database).

7. **Witness statement from security**: Guard notes C appeared "visibly upset, maybe crying" when leaving at 10:45 PM. Guard assumed a bad phone call.

A feels crushing guilt: they tried to destroy the victim professionally, and hours later the victim was physically destroyed. **Unlocking payoff:** When confronted with the complaint (C, as department head, was CC'd on the board distribution list and noticed; or B may have seen internal communications while snooping), A breaks down: "I was trying to protect people! I didn't think anything of it until I heard about... everything. C went in there after me."

## Gameplay

### Chain of Deduction 1: The Romantic Thread

1. **Opening**: Player reviews evidence. Notes the victim's text mentioning love (❤️) to a prepaid phone. The disclosure form is intriguing but the partner field is blank. Who was the victim meeting?

2. **Interview E** (routine questioning) → E claims professional relationship only. But E seems to know a lot about C's meetings with the victim. Why would E track that?

3. **Interview D** → D mentions "drama" around the victim. When pushed, D reveals C has been "very attentive" to the victim for months - inappropriate touching, jealous interruptions.

4. **Interview C** → C says victim was "preoccupied," always rushing off. C observes that E seems to be taking the death "harder than expected." When asked about the flirty email, C deflects.

5. **Press D further** about C → D reveals following C back to the building, seeing C emerge from Deep Lab looking devastated. Also mentions seeing someone enter via executive parking around 10:50.

6. **Confront C** with D's observation and the email pattern → C's composure breaks. Confesses to the year-long obsession and manipulation campaign. Reveals victim was expecting someone they loved that night - someone they were going to "make it official" with. C suspects it was E.

7. **Return to E** with C's revelation → Describe the manipulation campaign. Watch E's reaction - recognition, fury, grief. E begins to realize they were played.

8. **Request executive parking logs** through security → E entered at 10:50 PM. No exit on that system.

9. **Final confrontation with E** → Present complete picture: the manipulation C orchestrated, D seeing E enter, B confirming the garage entry via system monitoring, both C and A confirming victim was alive during their visits but expecting someone, the disclosure form being drafted. E confesses.

### Chain of Deduction 2: The Timeline Thread

1. **Opening**: Player notes B left extremely late (11:55 PM on badge). B claims 11:30. Also, A returned at 9:30 PM for just "glasses" but stayed 35 minutes.

2. **Interview A** → A's account doesn't match the Deep Lab access log. When pressed, A admits being in the Deep Lab with the victim. A also mentions B seemed "jumpy" at the party, and overheard victim expressing betrayal about someone recently.

3. **Confront B** with the timeline discrepancy and the accusation → B, cornered, confesses to being a spy. In exchange for cooperation, B reveals: saw C rushing to Deep Lab around 10:35 and leaving distraught at 10:45. Also noticed through system monitoring that someone with executive parking access entered at 10:50. Also saw D lurking near the Deep Lab area around 11 PM.

4. **This establishes**: Multiple people visited victim in Deep Lab that night. Victim was alive when C left at 10:40 (both A and B can confirm C's emotional state). Someone with executive access entered at 10:50.

5. **Interview D** → D admits following C, confirms seeing C leave distraught. Also admits seeing someone enter from executive parking around 10:50 - thinks it might have been E based on body language.

6. **D also reveals** suspicion that victim and E were secretly together (saw them in the same car). And mentions E seems to know too much about C's behavior toward victim.

7. **Review disclosure form** → Victim was drafting it the night of the murder. Clearly intended for someone special. The loving text was sent at 10:48 PM.

8. **Interview E about the relationship** → E denies, then admits when confronted with D's observations and the victim's text. Claims to have been home.

9. **Request executive parking records** → E entered at 10:50 PM. E was the last person to see the victim alive.

10. **Final confrontation** → E was there. E was the person the victim was waiting for. E's jealousy of C (which C deliberately manufactured) reached critical mass. Murder window is 10:55-11:10 PM. E confesses.

## Critique

### Improvements from v3

1. **Changed location to "Deep Lab"**: Basement level, RF-shielded, restricted access. This is more plausible than a repurposed meditation room. The technical justification (RF shielding for AI testing) makes it feel like a real space. The restricted keycard access adds an additional investigative element - who had access?

2. **Made the HR disclosure form ambiguous**: The partner field is now BLANK instead of having E's name. The document was last edited at 10:52 PM but not saved in final form. This removes the immediate finger-pointing at E and requires the player to deduce who the victim was meeting.

3. **Added B's system monitoring capability**: As a spy, B was monitoring internal systems. This gives B access to information about the executive parking entry - a more believable way to get this evidence than just requesting logs. It also reinforces B's espionage expertise.

4. **Improved D's witness value**: D now directly sees someone entering from executive parking around 10:50 and specifically suspects it was E based on body language. This is stronger testimony that doesn't require system access.

5. **Strengthened the cross-connections**: 
   - A sees C arriving
   - B sees C leaving distraught AND monitors the garage entry
   - C knows the victim was expecting someone they loved
   - D sees C leaving AND sees executive parking entry AND suspects the E relationship
   - Everyone's testimony interlocks more tightly

6. **Changed murder weapon to scarf**: The victim's burgundy silk scarf is more distinctive than a generic necktie. It's something the victim "always wore to important occasions" - they wore it to the celebration. This small detail makes the crime feel more personal and poignant.

7. **Tightened the murder window**: Now clearly 10:55-11:10 PM. E enters at 10:50, reaches Deep Lab by 10:55, murder occurs around 11:05, E flees by 11:10. Much narrower than v3.

8. **Added the victim's marginal note**: The email from C has the victim's handwritten note: "Weird. C being strange again." This shows the victim noticed C's behavior was inappropriate but didn't understand it was a manipulation campaign. Adds pathos - the victim was aware something was wrong but couldn't see the full picture.

### Remaining Strengths

1. **Tragic core preserved**: E killed their lover based on jealousy C manufactured. The victim was about to go public with their love. The emotional impact remains devastating.

2. **C's moral responsibility intact**: C didn't commit murder but created the conditions for it. Satisfying moral complexity.

3. **Connected clue network**: Every secret unlocks through multiple paths. No isolated information islands.

4. **Realistic conversation hooks**: The gameplay chains use natural questions ("Why were you really there that night?" "Tell me about your relationship with the victim").

5. **Escalating revelations**: Each unlocked secret provides new information about others.
